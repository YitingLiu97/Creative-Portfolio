A Day, Any Day, Everyday

A live audiovisual performance echoing the efforts we make living through the experience where days are no longer days and nights are no longer nights.

Highlights

This project was created during PerformaJam sponsored by ITP-NYU Adjacent Journal in collaboration with CultureHubNYC. In collaboration with Edwin Oliver-Rose, we translated our feelings during COVID-19 into audio visual performance within under 6-hour. We used personal footage of us doing mundane things, screen recordings as visuals and sourced daily sounds and audios around us.

Skill: Ableton Live, Recording, Remixing, Audition, Live Performance

Time: Made within 6 hours on Apr. 25, 2020.

Role

I created the sound piece while Oliver-Rose made the visuals.

Problem Statement

How might we translate our feelings of voidness and lack of energy into an audiovisual live performance?

Here are some stills from our live performance.


Concept

During the PerformaJam, we were given the concept of “Energy Vampire”, which creates the sense of energy draining and the realm of voidness during COVID-19. We brainstormed and researched on social media platforms and discovered the complaint from most of them that everyday feels the same. In order to highlight that as our concept, we included elements that loop the visuals and music to create a sense that days are no longer days, nights are no longer nights.
